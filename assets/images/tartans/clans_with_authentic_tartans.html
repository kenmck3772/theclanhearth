Clan MacDonald

"Per mare per terras" (By sea and by land)

Lords of the Isles

Gaelic Name: Mac Dhòmhnaill (Son of Donald)
Territory: Isles of Scotland, Skye, Uist
Clan Chief: High Chief of Clan Donald

The MacDonalds are the senior clan of Scotland, descended from Somerled, King of the Isles. For centuries they ruled as Lords of the Isles, commanding vast territories and fleets of galleys across the Hebrides and western Scotland.

Famous Members:

  • Somerled - King of the Isles (12th century)
  • John MacDonald, 1st Lord of the Isles
  • Flora MacDonald - Jacobite heroine

Clan Campbell

"Ne obliviscaris" (Forget not)

Dukes of Argyll

Gaelic Name: Clann Diarmaid (Children of Diarmid)
Territory: Argyll, Inveraray Castle
Clan Chief: Duke of Argyll

One of Scotland's most powerful clans, the Campbells rose to prominence through strategic alliances and military prowess. They became the premier Highland clan supporting the government and Protestant cause.

Famous Members:

  • Archibald Campbell, 1st Marquess of Argyll
  • John Campbell, 2nd Duke of Argyll
  • Colin Campbell, 1st Baron Clyde

Clan Fraser

"Je suis prest" (I am ready)

Lords of Lovat

Gaelic Name: Clann Friseal (The Frasers)
Territory: Inverness-shire, Lovat
Clan Chief: Lord Lovat

The Frasers of Lovat are one of the great Highland clans, known for their fierce independence and warrior tradition. They played crucial roles in Scottish history, from the Wars of Independence to Culloden.

Famous Members:

  • Simon Fraser, 11th Lord Lovat ("The Fox")
  • Simon Fraser the Patriot
  • Hugh Fraser, 1st Lord Lovat

Clan MacLeod

"Hold Fast"

Chiefs of Skye

Gaelic Name: Clann Mhic Leòid (Children of MacLeod)
Territory: Isle of Skye, Dunvegan Castle
Clan Chief: MacLeod of MacLeod

The MacLeods have held Dunvegan Castle for over 800 years, making it the longest continuously inhabited castle in Scotland. They are renowned seafarers and warriors of the Western Isles.

Famous Members:

  • Alasdair Crotach MacLeod (16th century chief)
  • Dame Flora MacLeod (28th Chief)
  • Norman MacLeod (19th century preacher)

Clan Stewart

"Virescit vulnere virtus" (Courage grows strong at a wound)

Royal House of Scotland

Gaelic Name: Stiùbhart (Steward)
Territory: Throughout Scotland
Clan Chief: The Queen (House of Windsor-Stewart line)

The Stewart name derives from the hereditary position of High Steward of Scotland. The family rose to become the royal dynasty of Scotland, and later Great Britain, ruling for over 300 years.

Famous Members:

  • Robert II - First Stewart King of Scotland
  • Mary Queen of Scots
  • James VI & I - United Scottish and English crowns
  • Bonnie Prince Charlie - The Young Pretender

Clan Gordon

"Bydand" (Remaining/Steadfast)

Marquesses of Huntly

Gaelic Name: Clann Ghòrdain (Clan Gordon)
Territory: Aberdeenshire, Huntly Castle
Clan Chief: Marquess of Huntly

The Gordons rose to become one of the most powerful families in the Northeast, known as the "Cock o' the North." They dominated Aberdeenshire and produced many distinguished soldiers and statesmen.

Famous Members:

  • George Gordon, 4th Earl of Huntly
  • General Charles Gordon ("Gordon of Khartoum")
  • Lord Byron - The famous poet

Understanding Scottish Tartans

Tartan Meanings

Red: Courage, strength, bravery in battle
Green: The Highland landscape, growth, harmony
Blue: Loyalty, truth, the Scottish seas and lochs
Yellow/Gold: Honor, generosity, the crown
Purple: Nobility, justice, royal blood
Black: Determination, strength, the night

Tartan Types

Clan Tartans

Traditional patterns associated with specific Scottish clans

Dress Tartans

Formal versions, often with white backgrounds

Hunting Tartans

Muted colors for camouflage during hunts

Ancient vs Modern

Ancient uses older, faded dyes; Modern uses bright, contemporary colors
